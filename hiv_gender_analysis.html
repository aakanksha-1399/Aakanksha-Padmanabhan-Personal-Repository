Living with HIV vs. New Infections

Data shows that while the number of adolescents living with HIV remains high, the rate of new infections has not grown proportionally in recent years. This suggests improved survival rates, likely due to expanded access to antiretroviral therapy (ART), increased awareness, and better healthcare interventions. However, it also underscores the need for sustained treatment access and targeted prevention programs, especially for vulnerable groups like adolescent girls.

Results

This project highlights the persistent gender disparities and regional trends in adolescent HIV infection. Through data-driven insights and interactive visualizations, it underscores the need for targeted public health interventions, especially for adolescent girls who remain disproportionately affected. As healthcare policies evolve, continuous monitoring of these patterns can guide more equitable and effective responses to the global HIV crisis.
