Manhattan Traffic Insights

Python Pandas Data Cleaning Seaborn Visualization Urban Planning

Project Overview

This project analyzes Manhattan traffic accident data from 2019 and 2020 to uncover spatial and temporal patterns using clustering and exploratory techniques. We applied DBSCAN for hotspot detection and compared accident trends before and during the pandemic. The focus was on data cleaning, preprocessing, and extracting insights for urban planning and safety improvements.

Data Overview

This project leveraged a comprehensive dataset of New York City traffic accidents, focusing exclusively on the Manhattan borough to deliver targeted insights. The raw data required extensive cleaning due to numerous missing values and redundant columns, such as combined location data and separate injury/fatality counts, which were consolidated for clarity.


  • Date and Time: Enabled detailed temporal analysis of accident patterns by day and hour.
  • Zip Codes: Facilitated regional breakdowns to identify high-risk areas within Manhattan.
  • Geographic Coordinates (Latitude & Longitude): Supported spatial clustering to uncover accident hotspots.
  • Injury and Fatality Counts: Provided severity metrics for each incident.

The dataset was quantized by temporal (day, hour) and spatial (zip code) dimensions, allowing for granular analysis of trends and clusters. Despite some missing and unspecified data points, the preprocessing steps ensured a clean and robust dataset suitable for advanced clustering and visualization techniques.


This data preparation phase highlights my ability to handle messy real-world data, perform meaningful feature selection, and ready the data for insightful analytics.

Shift in Accident Hotspots Due to COVID-19 Behavior

Clustering with DBSCAN revealed a stark shift in accident hotspots between 2019 and 2020. In 2019, clusters concentrated around tourist destinations like Hudson Yards (The Vessel, The Edge) indicated heavy footfall and travel activity. However, in 2020, post-lockdown, accident density increased in residential and restaurant-heavy areas like Lexington Avenue â€” a trend likely driven by increased food delivery traffic during stay-at-home periods.

Peak Accident Times During the Day

Analysis of accident data by hour reveals a notable spike around 3 PM. This peak likely corresponds to increased traffic during lunch hours, when many people are on the road after eating and may experience drowsiness. Additionally, this time coincides with a surge in food delivery activities, which contributes to higher vehicle density and, consequently, more accidents. Understanding this pattern helps identify critical time windows for traffic safety interventions.

Visualization of accident time of the day

Weekday Accident Patterns

Examining accidents across days of the week shows that Fridays consistently experience the highest number of collisions. This increase can be attributed to end-of-week rushes, social events, and gatherings that contribute to heavier traffic volumes and potentially riskier driving behavior. Recognizing this weekly trend enables targeted planning for traffic control and public safety measures on the busiest days.
